Precision Agriculture

"Precision agriculture is a smart farming approach that uses GPS, drones, sensors, and data analytics to manage crops more efficiently. By applying the right inputs at the right place and time, it helps farmers boost yields, save resources, and protect the environment."

Site Specific Nutrient Management

"Site-Specific Management focuses on treating different parts of a field according to their unique needs. By using technologies like GPS, sensors, and GIS, farmers can apply the right amount of water, fertilizer, and pesticides where they’re needed most—boosting efficiency, saving costs, and protecting the environment."

Satellite and Drone for Agriculture

"Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs), or drones, are transforming agriculture by providing high-resolution images and real-time data on crop health, soil conditions, and irrigation needs. They enable farmers to monitor fields efficiently, detect problems early, and apply resources precisely—improving productivity while reducing costs and environmental impact."

ICT in Agriculture

Information and Communication Technology (ICT) has become a cornerstone of modern agriculture, bridging the gap between farmers, researchers, and markets. By leveraging digital tools, mobile applications, internet connectivity, and data management systems, ICT empowers farmers to make informed decisions, improve productivity, and enhance sustainability. The integration of ICT into agriculture is revolutionizing traditional practices and enabling smarter, more efficient farming.
